# Task Description
**CoT Prompt Design:**

"Construct a *multi-perspective forensic analysis* of the event by adopting the mindset of a linguistic detective reconstructing a scene from textual evidence. Begin with: **'Let’s reconstruct this event through layered scrutiny.'** Follow this protocol:

1. **Temporal-Causal Blueprint**:  
   - First, identify the *trigger* and situate it within a micro-timeline: what immediately precedes or follows it in the sentence? How does this sequence inform causality or agency?  
   - Map how the trigger’s semantic force (e.g., violent, transactional, communicative) constrains plausible roles.

2. **Entity Interrogation Matrix**:  
   - For each **entity**, evaluate it through three lenses:  
     (a) *Syntactic position* relative to the trigger (subject, object, prepositional modifier),  
     (b) *Semantic compatibility* with the *template* roles (e.g., can a *location* serve as an *attacker*?),  
     (c) *World knowledge plausibility* (e.g., can an organization *die*?).  
   - **Explicitly reject** any entity-role pairing that fails any lens, citing the specific reason.

3. **Contrastive Role Assignment**:  
   - When multiple entities could plausibly fill a role (e.g., two people near a *conflict* trigger), compare them directly: which has stronger grammatical or contextual anchoring to the trigger?  
   - Justify why one is assigned the role while the other is excluded—even if both seem superficially relevant.

4. **Narrative Coherence Check**:  
   - After provisional assignments, synthesize a mini-narrative using the *event_type* and filled *template*. Does it form a logically coherent event? If not, revisit assignments.

5. **Final Validation**:  
   - Cross-check every assigned *role* against the official role definitions implied by the *event_type*. Ensure no role is left unfilled if the sentence provides sufficient evidence—and no role is invented beyond the template.

Throughout, embed **bolded entities** and *italicized roles* in your reasoning. Anchor every claim to specific words or phrases in the *sentence*. Your output must be 150–500 words, blending structural rigor with interpretive depth."

## Requirements:
- Start with a natural intro (e.g., *"Alright, let’s break down..."*)
- Use **bold/italics** to emphasize key entities/roles.
- Avoid generic phrases—**every step must reference the input’s specifics**.
- Keep the chain between 150-500 words.

## Example Output Format:
```  
Alright, let's tackle this problem step-by-step.
1. [Analysis step 1]
2. [Analysis step 2]
......
n. [Conclusion]
```
